The interactive bar chart race visualization presents a comprehensive overview of the global rankings of ports worldwide. Through a captivating bar chart race, we can observe the consistent presence of Shenzhen and Guangzhou among the top 20 ports in terms of throughput.

Guangzhou, a renowned port of great historical significance since the Tang Dynasty, has a profound legacy in international trade. It has established extensive global trade routes connecting Europe, Latin America, South Asia, the East, and Oceania. Notably, it stands as the only surviving maritime Silk Road port from the Qing government's closed-door policy era.
Notably, the rise of the virtual economy has also contributed to Guangzhou's progress in international trade, as evidenced by the fact that Guangzhou has ranked first in the country's airports in terms of cross-border e-commerce business for seven consecutive years.
Shenzhen's Qianhai Free Trade Zone has brought about significant transformations, injecting fresh vitality into the city's industrial advancement. The integration of financial, trade, and shipping services in Qianhai fosters Shenzhen's industrial growth and facilitates closer collaboration with Hong Kong. This, in turn, enhances the seamless convergence of Shenzhen's Qianhai district with Hong Kong and aligns the practices with international standards.

This line graph shows how the total amount of foreign direct investment(FDI) utilised by different cities in the Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area has changed over a 30-year period. While 80% of the cities in the Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area experienced an upward trend in foreign direct investment between 1990 and 2020, Jiangmen, Zhongshan and Zhaoqing have a low potential to attract foreign investment due to their long reliance on traditional manufacturing industries. Guangzhou, the capital of Guangdong Province, led in terms of actual FDI until 2002, when it was overtaken by the newly-emerged Special Economic Zone, Shenzhen.
