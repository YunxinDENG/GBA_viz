The map demonstrates the population distribution and population density in each district in the nine cities in the Greater Bay Area and their trends of change in the past decade.
The map is visualised based on the data from China population 2020, WorldPop

From the distribution and height of the 3D extrusion bars, it can be known that almost all areas have been experiencing population growth over the past ten years. Guangzhou has the highest population density of all time, and Shenzhen is also very densely populated, second to Guangzhou. The density of the population varies from high to low, radiating out from the Pearl River Estuary.

This 100% vertical bar chart depicts the differences in the percentages of employees across the three sectors of the economy in each city of the Greater Bay Area from 2011 to 2021.
The chart is visualised based on the data from Guangdong Statistical Yearbook.

It can be observed that the primary industry, which mainly involves the development of natural resources such as agriculture, has had a consistently low and decreasing percentage of employees. The secondary industry which majorly features manufacturing, has also witnessed a decline but to a lesser degree. The tertiary industry, which encompasses the service sector, has experienced substantial changes, with an increase in the proportion of employees across all cities.

Most of the cities have been witnessing a similar trend of changes, although there are differences in industry structure due to different focuses on the development of the cities. For example, Shenzhen has a rather strong innovative development, while Zhaoqing and Zhongshan are still heavily relying on traditional manufacturing and agriculture.

The area chart presents a more specific and detailed categorisation of the labour force of 18 economic sectors for each city from 2011 to 2020, including both proportions and numbers.
The chart is visualised based on the data from Guangdong Statistical Yearbook .

For all the cities in the chart, the manufacturing industry has the largest proportion of all economic sectors. However, the employee distribution in each city has its own characteristics. For example, for Zhaoqing and Jiangmen, education has a relatively large proportion of employees compared to other cities.

For Guangzhou,the labour force distribution in different economic sectors is relatively equal, while other cities tend to have a much more manufacturing-intensive labour force distribution. It is also noticeable that due to the significant difference in population between Guangzhou, Shenzhen and other cities, they also have much more labour force distributed in most of the sectors than other cities, although the proportion seems to be low and the numbers are not directly compared on the area chart.
